    Frequently Asked
    Questions

    Everything you need to know about CAC registration in Nigeria.

    What is CAC and why do I need to register?+
    CAC (Corporate Affairs Commission) is the Nigerian government body responsible for registering businesses and organizations. Registration gives your business legal recognition, lets you open a corporate bank account, bid for contracts, and builds trust with customers and partners.
    What's the difference between Business Name and LLC?+
    A Business Name (Sole Proprietorship) is owned by one person and is simpler and cheaper to register. An LLC is a separate legal entity requiring at least 2 directors, offering stronger legal protection and more credibility for businesses seeking investment or contracts.
    How long does registration take?+
    Business Name: 3–5 working days. LLC: 7–14 working days. Incorporated Trustees (NGO/Church): 3–6 weeks due to CAC's mandatory newspaper publication requirement.
    Do I need to visit any office in person?+
    No — everything is 100% remote. Fill the form online, upload or send documents via WhatsApp, and receive your certificate by email.
    What documents do I need for Business Name registration?+
    You need: (1) Valid government-issued photo ID — NIN slip, International Passport, Driver's Licence, or Voter's Card. (2) Recent passport photograph with white background. (3) Proof of address — utility bill not older than 3 months. (4) Signature specimen on plain white paper.
    Can my preferred business name be rejected?+
    Yes — CAC rejects names already taken, too similar to existing names, or potentially misleading. That's why we ask for 3 name options. You can also use our Name Search service to verify beforehand.
    What is a PSC (Person with Significant Control)?+
    A PSC holds more than 25% of shares or voting rights, or exercises significant influence over a company. Under CAMA 2020, companies must identify and register their PSCs with CAC. Most majority shareholders and founders qualify as PSCs.
    What is Incorporated Trustees and who needs it?+
    It's the CAC registration for non-profit organizations — churches, mosques, NGOs, charities, community groups, and professional associations. It requires at least 2 trustees, a constitution, and a newspaper publication notice.
    What happens after I submit the form?+
    You receive a unique reference number immediately and can upload your documents directly from the confirmation screen. Our team contacts you on WhatsApp (usually within a few hours on business days) to confirm next steps.
    Do I need to file annual returns after registration?+
    Yes. CAC requires all registered businesses to file annual returns every year. Failure attracts penalties and can lead to delisting. Use our Annual Returns Tracker section to track your deadline and get automatic reminders.
    Is my personal information safe with you?+
    Absolutely. Your personal details and documents are used solely for CAC registration and handled with strict confidentiality. We do not share your information with any third party other than the CAC portal as required for filing.
    When do I pay, and what payment methods are accepted?+
    Payment is made after our team confirms your application via WhatsApp. We accept bank transfers and mobile payments. Our fees cover the full registration process. Contact us on WhatsApp for a current price quote.
